General Information about Slope Deflection Method

The slope deflection method is a structural analysis technique used to analyze and solve statically indeterminate beams and frames. It involves calculating the slopes and deflections at various points along the structure, considering the effects of bending and axial loads.

Steps for Analyzing Beams and Frames:

For Beams:

  1. Identify the support conditions and external loads.
  2. Calculate fixed-end moments at each end of the beam.
  3. Apply slope deflection equations for each segment of the beam.
  4. Solve the simultaneous equations to determine unknown displacements.
  5. Calculate member end moments and reactions.

For Frames (Sway and Non-Sway):

  1. Identify the support conditions and external loads.
  2. Calculate fixed-end moments at each end of the members.
  3. Apply slope deflection equations for each member.
  4. Formulate the equilibrium equations and solve for member end moments.
  5. Calculate support reactions and member forces.
